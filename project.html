Project Plan/Description 

App Name

PetWaze

Overview

Development of a Progressive Web App (PWA) that provides dog owners with quick information and reviews of road rest stops. Initially, the PWA will target Victorian roads, including rest stop information, reviews and a linked map. Future features to be included in the PWA include expanding rest stop information for other Australian states and territories and integrating with online map providers such as Google Maps. Integration with smart city technology platforms such as Snap Send Solve that simplifies community issue reporting to local councils and businesses.

Motivation

Michael Addison experienced the initial motivation for the project idea. After a breakdown on a Victorian Highway, Michael spent some time pulled over at a rest stop. He encountered a couple travelling with their large dog who pulled in to use the facilities during this time. There was nowhere safe for the dog to roam freely or access safe drinking water. After quick research on Google, Michael found that the information on facilities available to dog owners couldn’t be found regarding rest stops along major highways.

According to the ‘Pets in Australia’ survey conducted in 2019 by Newsgate Research on behalf of Animal Medicines Australia, 39.9% of Australian households are home to at least one dog. Of those dog owners, 25% report taking their dog on a road trip or holiday. This is a significant market opportunity for dog owners who would find this information helpful in planning their trips.

With the initial data feed to be supplied in conjunction with support from Visit Victoria, there are further opportunities for local councils and small businesses to receive preferred preferencing on the PWA to entice dog owners to stop at their facility. There will also be user feedback available at each rest stop giving dog owners the chance to directly engage and leave reviews on their experience to increase the services and upkeep in these areas.

Landscape

Similar products available would be Google Maps or Waze. These products provide detailed information about geographical regions and sites around the world. They allow you to orient yourself in said regions and plan trips or stops along the way. Competitors would be Bing Maps Citymapper which offers similar services, give or take some features. The difference is that this project will be more specific in its intention. It will enable the user to access basic information and photos and, more specifically, the availability of safe drinking water, areas for dogs to walk or run, and ratings. Thus, allowing the user to plan trips with their pets.

Detailed Description

Aim

The PetWaze project aims to create and release a Progressive Web App (PWA) for Australian pet owners. The PWA can be installed on both Android and Apple iOS and minimises the need to publish a native app to the respective app stores while eliminating the need for the project team to learn and develop two separate apps in different.  

One of the main goals is to create a PWA Figma design that the project team can reference. Both frontend and backend developers can use the design in both static and prototype views to aid in developing the PWA. Project team members testing the PWA can use the design to determine expected versus actual behaviour and ensure the developed PWA meets its designed functional requirements.

Other goals include developer documentation on setting up their respective development environments and process documentation on how to publish a PWA. Further technical documentation needed includes how app features were architected, which is helpful if a project team member leaves the project and new developers begin on the project.

Plans and Progress

As many people would love to go on road trips, long drives or travel interstate with their pets, there is currently no platform, app or website that provides pet owners with information to find facilities that cater to their needs. This is the inspiration behind PetWaze, it will be created as a PWA, as PWAs can easily be accessed via multiple browsers and devices, do not require downloads, and can be easily shared and accessed, which caters to the needs PetWaze’s users. The use of PWA technology does provide possible insight into what possible growth PetWaze may receive. Large online retailer AliExpress reported their engagement had drastically increased after transitioning to PWA “AliExpress saw a 104% increase in conversion rates for new users once they transitioned to a PWA approach.” (What is a PWA? Pros and Cons of a PWA - Builder.ai®, 2022). PetWaze has no competitors or foundational research as it’s a new niche.

Currently, we’ve decided not to make any changes to PetWaze. The main concern is to create a PWA that can supply information solely for Victorians, this will consist of a map, rest stop information and reviews. The lack of change with the PetWaze is that it is a niche that doesn’t have any current competitors that could supply valuable insights. This PWA is meant to be the first of its kind, so it’s more sensible to focus on creating a stable foundation for PetWaze’s longevity. The possible changes that could be implemented to this PWA are its future features that would include integrating with established map providers such as Google Maps and OpenStreetMaps and incorporating and adding information from other Australian states and territories. The current stage of PetWaze is to design its PWA wireframe and user interface by utilising Figma to create the prototype. 

The two aspects that have contributed to this progression are the current understanding of PWA in relation to the needs of PetWaze and the tools Figma supplies to create a functional web app. A beneficial feature of PWA technology is the security it provides to both users and developers as stated, “Progressive Web Apps are served via HTTPS, which ensures that without authentication it cannot be tampered by anyone.” (Impact of progressive web apps on web app development. International Journal of Innovative Research in Science, Engineering and Technology). PWAs must adhere to HTTPS guidelines which provide the PWA with security, since PWAs are also accessed through web browsers as well, these web browsers have built-in security features which aid in providing added security. PetWaze must be accessible to all regardless of device, with the utilisation of PWA technology will enhance accessibility and discoverability. As PWAs are web-based applications, no installation of downloads is needed. This makes PetWaze accessible to users with slower connection speed, smaller storage space, or slower speed devices. Potential PetWaze users who plan to travel to remote areas will not have to be concerned about accessibility. As the PetWaze is intended for users who travel, phone battery conservation is extremely important. The PWA falls short of preserving the battery and consumes more energy than native apps, this is caused by the complex codes needed for PWAs. Another concern is that PWAs have fewer functions, especially for PetWaze users with Apple devices. This may lead to limited user experience, and users may not be able to use Bluetooth, Siri, and push notifications. We’ve chosen Figma to create the PetWaze because it allows us to utilise multiple design tools and add the required plug-ins to the PWA. As Figma is a cloud-based designing platform, this enables the PetWaze to be built collaboratively in real-time without the need for members needing to depend on one individual to design the PWA. This PWA does require a map and review feature, Figma APIs allow for easy integration of Google Maps and reviews. This gives for both easy design control and user access. Another benefit is that it supports and provides all the tools required to make the prototype design and convert them into a fully functional responsive website. A more technical concern of utilising Figma for “PetWaze” is that it does require that a patron of “PetWaze” needs to have a 4GB of ram and Intel HD 3000 graphic card. If users do not have this, it can make accessing the PWA more complicated and may take longer to view the PWA.

The potential end goal of Petwaze is a fully functional PWA, which allows users to register an account, view reviews, access the map to find rest stops, and more. Beyond depending on a PWA, another possible future progression for PetWaze is that it becomes available on app stores to be accessed either as PWA or a native app. This possible progression is that PWA features are not fully accessible on certain devices, such as Apple, as they have not yet integrated PWA technology with their IOS software.

As PetWaze aims to be available to all users, it must have a native app for optimal convenience in the future. It’s recommended that PetWaze developers have a team of at least a project manager, backend, frontend, and UX/UI designer to successfully continue developing PetWaze. To guarantee success, it’s essential that the project manager can plan the future progression of PetWaze thoroughly to meet deadlines. They must be able to create plans that comply with scopes, timeframes, and budgets. PetWaze must have a frontend developer who has the technical experience to develop and maintain the user interfaces and create new features that improve site interaction. The foundation of PetWaze is the backend developer who will be expected to create a sustainable web app and can understand cross-platform functionality and compatibility. The backend developer must build and maintain the database, ensuring that PetWaze has high performance and responsiveness. As PetWaze is aimed to be accessible to users, the UX/UI designer needs to empathise with PetWaze users’ needs to design user-friendly interfaces. They could continue developing PetWaze through Microsoft’s PWABuilder. This provides the developers with the necessary tools to build the PetWaze and simple PWA to native app conversion.  

Roles

The roles required to create the PetWaze consist of the project manager, whose responsibility is to make sure that they plan and propose the projects and goals in detail and stick to a timeline and budget. The backend and frontend developers need to work congruently to successfully create a functional, user-friendly web app and main the web server and database. With the future progression of the PetWaze, this role can eventually become just a Full Stack Developer, as they are proficient and have expertise in both Frontend and Backend web development. Considering that PetWaze is heavily user-based, UX/UI designers are crucial as their responsibilities consist of researching user experience and designing high-quality design solutions through wireframes and prototypes. For guaranteed success in creating the PetWaze consistent collaboration is required. In the future, when PetWaze is more established, then the roles can be rotated, presently, collaboration is critical within the first few months to a year to secure optimal design and usage.  

Scope and Limits

The PWA will feature information and reviews of road rest stops. It will primarily target Victorian roads and include information, reviews, and a linked map. Due to limitations, these will be the set priorities and expansion to further features will not be feasible in the timeframe given. These future features would include integrating the PWA into other providers such as Google Maps and Snap Send to expand to other states and territories and provide issue reporting to local councils and businesses.

Testing

Michael has decided that Test Driven Development (TDD) using the JEST framework will be used for the code testing part of the project. This is an iterative approach that combines the three below steps:

  1. Create precise tests to verify the functionality of a specific feature
  2. Correcting the code once a test fails so it can run successfully
  3. Refactor the code to ensure it’s optimised and DRY 

Chromium, Firefox and Safari all provide browser-inbuilt developer tools. These will be used to assist with testing key PWA metrics such as service works, JSON manifests and internet connection throttling to name a few.

Google Lighthouse will also be used periodically just the development process to conduct site audits, listing metrics important to UI and UX.
